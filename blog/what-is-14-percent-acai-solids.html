Understanding the Grading System

Açaí berries grow wild in the Amazon floodplain, harvested primarily for their dense pulp. Once processed into puree, the grade of the product is determined by its total solids percentage—meaning the actual concentration of fruit pulp versus water content.

Commercial suppliers typically offer varying grades, ranging from low-end commercial blends (often 7% to 9% solids) all the way up to premium heavy grades. Lower grades require massive amounts of fillers, ice, guar gum, or added juices just to achieve a scoopable texture.

Why Cutting Corners Destroys Nutrition

When a shop uses low-solids puree or reconstitutes açaí powder with water, you lose the core nutritional properties that made the berry a global superfood:

  • Fewer Anthocyanins: These are the potent plant pigments responsible for açaí’s deep purple hue and its elite antioxidant capacity, which fights cellular oxidative stress.
  • Missing Omega Fatty Acids: Real, high-solids açaí is naturally rich in healthy omegas (Omega 3, 6, and 9) that support heart health and sustained energy. Dilution strips these essential fats away.
  • Hidden Sugars: Because low-solids purees lack natural depth and richness, businesses often overcompensate with heavy syrups, processed cane sugars, or condensed milk.
